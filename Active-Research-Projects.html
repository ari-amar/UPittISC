Active Research Projects

University Of Pittsburgh

UPitt REPAIR

Description: ARPAR-E funded Rapid Encapsulation of Pipelines Avoiding Intensive Replacement (REPAIR) project at UPitt to develop in-situ repairs of pipelines and embedded intelligence. The technology has value-added enhanced corrosion protection and embedded sensing to complement existing non-destructive evaluation and in-line inspection techniques.

UPitt Nuclear Energy University Program

Description: Optical fiber and acoustic nondestructive evaluation (NDE) sensor technologies are being explored for dry cask storage monitoring applications by combining with advanced artificial intelligence and machine learning methods and physics-based models.

National Energy Technology Laboratory

Natural Gas Infrastructure FWP

Description: Sensor device and enabling materials are developed to enable increased reliability and resiliency of the natural gas infrastructure for safe and secure natural gas transport and delivery while also improving operational efficiency and abating environmental impacts from CH4 emission. Sensor technologies include advanced electrochemical sensor, optical fiber-based sensors (OFS), and passive wireless SAW-based sensors, with emphasis on internal corrosion monitoring and external CH4 leak detection.

Advanced Sensors and Controls FWP

Description: Optical fiber and SAW devices and enabling material technologies are being developed for high-temperature power plant applications.

Carbon Storage FWP

Description: Optical fiber-based CO2 sensors are being developed for monitoring of groundwater and geological sites for carbon sequestration.

United States (U.S.) Department of Energy (DOE) Grid Modernization Laboratory Consortium

Description: OFS are being developed and demonstrated for transformer, battery, and other electrical-asset monitoring applications.

Embedded Sensors for Wellbore Integrity Monitoring

Description: A suite of OFS and passive wireless-based sensors are being developed for monitoring wellbore integrity conditions with a focus on chemical sensing (e.g. pH and corrosivity).

Hydrogen Subsurface Storage FWP

Description: A suite of sensor technologies will be developed for real-time monitoring of microbial conversion in methane-hydrogen blend, gas composition, and geochemical parameters (e.g. pH) in the H2 storage wells, as well as wellbore integrity monitoring for H2 subsurface storage, including physical (e.g. temperature, stress, acoustic, vibration) and chemical (e.g. pH, corrosion, gas) parameters for cement and casing steel structural health monitoring.

Onshore Unconventional Resources FWP

Description: Develop an integrated network of sensor technologies for real-time monitoring of plugged abandoned well integrity to ensure effective and secure plugging and geologic zonal isolation.
