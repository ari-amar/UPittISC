Active Research Projects

University Of Pittsburgh

Advanced Sensor Development Laboratory

Description:State-of-the-art test equipment and multiple years’ expertise have been established at NETL R&IC for optical fiber sensor and wireless SAW sensor fabrication, interrogation, and chemical sensor layer synthesis. A reel-to-reel system and electroless plating system can demonstrate scalability of coating procedures for fiber sensors.

Center for Advanced Functional Materials Design

Description:NETL has a suite of advanced material laboratories for facilitating the syntheses/characterization of polymer and metal-organic framework (MOF) components, oxides and inorganic materials, metallic thin films, and other functional sensing layers to be leveraged for sensing material development and demonstration.

Distributed Optical Interrogator Development Program

Description:NETL has established a program to develop customized low-cost optical interrogators for structural health monitoring applications and to measure leakage and corrosion over a long-distance pipeline to aid in failure prediction and analysis. 

Subsurface Sensor Development Laboratory

Description:An automated HTHP system is built to simulate wellbore conditions for sensor development and materials durability testing. This system includes two batch reactors (500 mL and 1 L autoclaves) and one flow-through reactor. The HTHP reactors are rated up to 450°C and 6,500 psi, and currently are permitted to inject and hold both brine solutions and gas of CH4, CO2, air, N2, and H2S.

Electromagnetic Property Characterization and Testing Laboratory

Description:Facilities for characterizing electromagnetic properties of functional materials and devices, ranging from direct current to microwave and optical frequencies, including spectrophotometry, network analyzers, impedance analyzers, magnetometers, and physical property measurement systems.

Petersen Nanofabrication and Characterization Facility

Description:Facilities for nanofabrication of sensor devices including patterning, lithography, and thin film deposition as well as detailed characterization of functional sensor materials as well as fabricated sensor devices.  Facilities including sputtering, atomic layer deposition, evaporation, lithography, x-ray diffraction, scanning and transmission electron microscopy, ellipsometry, and a range of spectroscopy tools and techniques.

Magnetic, Electronic, and Photonic Materials and Device Laboratory

Description:Facilities for fabricating optical fiber and passive wireless sensor technologies and interrogation / telemetry techniques as well as sensor device testing and sensor material fabrication and characterization.

Single Crystal Fiber Fabrication and Characterization Facilities

Description:Facilities for fabricating and characterizing single crystal optical fibers using Laser Heated Pedestal Growth systems capable of extreme temperature and environment operation as well as novel functionality.
